Dormify

experience design, content design, user research

Dormify is an online website which aims to offer affordable furniture to people with small spaces while still helping them make their spaces aesthetically pleasing. For this 4-week project we were tasked with intervening in a problem area by constantly reframing of a problem statement. From this resulted 3 different solutions from 3 different reframes that we believed ultimately meets the need of the users.

RESEARCH AND FRAMING

To begin the process, we started by narrowing down our target audience to college students. This is was done to work within a small scope of the many audience dormify targeted. Plus, they were more easily accessible for our upcoming research.

Moving into college can be exciting yet nervous experience especially for students moving far away from home. In an informal conversation had by myself and a student, they expressed how important it is for their dorm rooms to feel like home, especially after a long day of class.


“I’ve never really been the one to care about the look of my room, but ever since moving into college that became an important aspect given I spend all my time there when I’m not in class.

- Student at SFU University


To further inform this, we conducted in-person interviews and a survey with 70 students. Our criteria for this was that they either have to be living in dorms or once did.

INSIGHT

From our research we discovered 3 problems that we found interesting

Interviewees stated that college life is an important experience for them and that their dorm room is part of that experience.

People do want to have a particular look and feel to their rooms but are unsure what exactly it is they want or how to achieve a feel and look to their dorms

FIRST FRAMING

FRAMING THE PROBLEM

We particularly found insight 2 more insightful and saw an opportunity to help students make their dorm rooms truly reflect who they are, and turn this regular space into their comfort space. So, we began to ask ourselves

How might we help college students in making better decisions about their dorm rooms?

SOLUTION 1

INTRODUCING THEMES

For our solution we created a space on the site where users can select room designs based on themes. This small but effective proposal, would help users deals with the overwhelming stress and question of “Where do I begin?” by organizing rooms into themes users can now easily browse room decor under a particular style.

SECOND FRAMING

REFRAMING THE PROBLEM

For our second reframe we decided to take a step back and tackle the problem of dorm room sizes centered around insight 3:

We also noted that there was a huge comparison between their rooms at home and the dorm room sizes with ultimately creates a problem of knowing how to properly integrate their personalities into the constraints of a dormitory

Dorm room sizes is an apparent issue, and with students looking to decorate their rooms to suit their taste, this poses a huge problem as they may not know how properly place items within their rooms making them unsatisfied with their rooms

In conducting further research we were interested in knowing how confident students felt in the utilization of their spaces, and if they care about how things are laid out within their spaces

RESULTS

68% of students as opposed to 32% feel they do know how to utilize their space properly.

How might we help students plan and understand their dorm rooms based on space constraints?

SOLUTION 2

ROOM VISUALIZER

For our second solution, we created a room visualizer to aid students in understanding space and understand how to make the most out of it. This will hopefully help them prepare better for when they move into dorm rooms
